How to Optimize your PC’s Performance


When it comes to tech, low performance is a real issue that is capable of hindering your work, ruining it your flow and perhaps push back progress.

Before rushing to the store to get new hardware or call the IT support, you might want to slow down and try out a few tricks first. In this guide, you will discover 6 important hacks you can do with your computer to optimize your performance.

Here is a list of all the fixes that we are going to highlight in this document:

1.​"Check Updates"

2.​Disable Startup Programs

3.​Run Storage Sense (Cleanup Files)

4.​Adjust Screen Appearance

5.​Check for Malware

1.​Check Updates

While you may think that your computer runs updates automatically, it might not always be the case. Sometimes you would have to check so for yourself. Unless you are on Windows 7 or even further downgraded, your computer is expected to receive consistent updates that will run bug fixes, provide hardware support and introduce Performance Improvements.

To check your updates, you need to do the following:

1.​Click on the Start Button.

2.​Click on the Settings Icon.

3.​Click on Update & Security.

4.​Then run Check for Updates. 

If you find out that you need to download updates, download them and then restart your computer. If you notice any difference, then running the updates was effective enough. However, if there were no more updates to install and your performance was still low, then you might want to check out the next step.


2.​Disable Startup Programs

If you open the task manager (right click on task bar > click on task manager), you will notice that there are applications that were open automatically once you have started your computer. They could have been set to automatically run once you start your computer, or you could have chosen to have them run during installation. Regardless, you can choose to disable startup programs through the following steps.

1.​Click on the Start Button.

2.​Click on the Settings Icon.

3.​Click on Applications.

4.​Click on Startup.

5.​Now choose which applications to disable.

6.​Restart your computer.

Once you restart your computer, open up the task manager again and check the list of applications. You will notice that the disabled applications will no longer be running in the background unless you start them up yourself. This way, your computer’s performance should be optimized.

However, if you notice that your performance is still low, then you should check the next step.


3.​Run Storage Sense (Cleanup Files)

Sometimes it is recommended that you do not keep temporary files in your computer (saved in downloads file or in recycling bin). However, as you may not always remember to delete them, windows has set up an option that automatically deletes temporary files according to a temporal cycle that you can set up yourself.

To access and activate Run Storage Sense, do the following:

1.​Click on the Start Button.

2.​Click on the Settings Icon.

3.​Click on System.

4.​Click on Storage.

5.​Click on Run Storage Sense.

6.​Then, you have the option to disable or enable the Run Storage Sense option, and you can select how much time passes before the temporary files are deleted automatically.

Once the temporary files are set to be deleted regularly, you might start noticing your computer’s performance elevating to a satisfactory level. With that said, there are other ways you can optimize it if you are not satisfied enough so far.


4.​Adjust Screen Appearance

While keeping your screen visually appealing can be regarded as a priority for you, it may not do too well with your computer’s performance. In order to maintain visual aesthetics, windows applies a series of visual effects, such as the following:

•​Minimizing and Maximizing windows, opening menus and transitions

•​Transparency effects, such as depth and layering

•​Smooth fonts

•​Shadow effects to icons

If you have tried the previous steps and still believe that your performance is slow, then you might want to rethink your screen’s visuals.

In order to switch from a pro-visual appeal to a pro-performance one, you need to check out the following steps:

1.​Access Control Panel: You can find it in the Search Bar 

2.​Click on System and Security.

3.​Click on System.

4.​Click on Advanced Settings.

5.​Click on Settings in the Performances Tab.

6.​In the Visual Effects tab, check the Adjust for best performance.

7.​Click on Apply.

8.​Restart your PC.

Once your restart, you will notice that your computer looks less visually attractive, yet feels potentially faster. At this point, most of the fixes that are run are responsive to internal issues with no external events. If the previous fixes did not seem to solve the issue, the next fix will definitely define your next approach.

5.​Check for Malware

According to GETASRA, there are over 1 billion malware programs in existence, and Trojans1 account for 58% of all computer malware. Besides, the study also indicates that 560 000 new pieces of malware are being detected every day. So, if fully operating companies are vulnerable to malware attacks, you might not tend to be so confident about your personal computer. In a way, unless you have sensitive data in your computer, you are less likely to be the target of a cyberattack. However, you would still be vulnerable to the threat nonetheless. Because the malware, particularly Trojans, runs in the background and consumes Processor and Ram usage the same as any other application, this could be the reason why your performance is so low.

Caution​It is always recommended that you have a reputable anti-virus installed in your computer. While it may not necessarily kill the threat, the likelihood of being infected and damaged will definitely be lower.

Assuming that you do not have an anti-virus, here are some quick hacks you can use to detect the malware and hopefully get rid of them.

1.​Access Windows Security (Start Button > Settings > Privacy and Security > Windows Security > Open Windows Security)

2.​Check Windows’ Virus & Threat Protection Updates (Select Virus & Threat Protection> Check for Updates under Virus & Threat Protection Updates)

3.​If you wish to make a scan, Run Quick Scan under Virus & Threat Protection.

4.​If:

a)​a virus is detected and removed/ quarantined, restart your computer.


b)​no virus is detected, restart your computer

c)​If a virus is detected but could not be removed/ quarantined, contact Window Support.

If you wish to conduct an offline scan, you may use Microsoft Windows Defender by following the steps below.

1.​Access Windows Security (Start Button > Settings > Privacy and Security > Windows Security > Open Windows Security)

2.​Check Windows’ Virus & Threat Protection Updates (Select Virus & Threat Protection> Scan Options under Current Threats > Microsoft Defender Antivirus)

3.​Your computer will automatically restart before Microsoft Defender Antivirus runs the scanning, after which a restart is scheduled.

6.​Conclusion

At this point, If you have tried all of the preceding steps to elevate your computer’s performance and push back slowness and the result remained, then perhaps solving the issue would either require a full reset of your computer or a full hardware inspection, which may lead to hardware change.

According to Ascendant, a laptop’s lifespan expectancy “The average lifespan of laptops varies: consumer models last 3 years, business models 5-7 years, and high-end models like Apple laptops can last up to 10 years.” So you should always bear in mind that the heavier the work you conduct with your device, the shorter its lifespan is supposed to be. Performance issues can be the result of machine nearing its lifespan.

However, before reaching such a conclusion, please try to apply the troubleshooting hacks in this document, and also consider a full reset of your computer.
